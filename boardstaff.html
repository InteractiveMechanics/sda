A Letter from Astrid Bennett

President of Surface Design Association

SDA advocates for excellence in textile-inspired art and design, but it is our community of members that enables SDA to carry forward that mission to the public. We are first and foremost, a community of makers whose work incorporates a wide range of techniques, materials and styles in textiles and new media. We explore the edges of current practice while also embracing the work of novices, students and emerging artists, collectors and those who are simply curious about the field.

Textiles and new media are experiencing a renaissance in the larger art world. We explore these in our quarterly Journal, now digital, in our national and regional exhibitions, and in our opportunities for members to create their own events and view sample collections. Whatever your interest in textiles and new media, please join us and add your voice to the conversation.

A Letter from Danielle Kelly

Executive Director of Surface Design Association

The studio experience can be a solitary one. The singular nature of a work space supports focus, rigor, and unfettered exploration; on our best days it catapults the mind to realize ideas in physical form.

But even the most dedicated practice craves community. For almost 4 decades, Surface Design Assocation has provided a platform for the exchange of ideas, methods, and materials. The SDA community is expansive: from makers and artists to academics and enthusiasts, celebrating their successes and failures as they work toward sustaining traditional techniques or adapting production strategies in radical new ways, realizing fiber arts as activism or performance. Their shared affinity is a sincere love for fiber and textile arts.

It is an honor to join SDA's vibrant community as the organization imagines its next 40 years of dynamic exchange and radical dedication to the ever evolving field of fiber and textile arts.
